Berkeley's Chapel of the Flowers

A Historic Venue in Berkeley

Located at 3049 Adeline Street, the Chapel of the Flowers offers a unique experience inside a 101-year-old storybook chapel. With convenient access to public transit and ample parking, it is an inspiring venue for a range of gatherings.

Gallery

History

Originally built in 1924, this chapel has served multiple purposes over the decades. It was once home to Marmot Mountain Works, the famed outdoor gear company, Terra's Temple, as well as Trackers Bay Area, an outdoor education organization. Nearby, Flaco's Tacos was a beloved staple in the community before its closure. Today, the chapel continues as a gathering place for spiritual, cultural, and artistic events.
