Berkeley's Chapel of the Flowers

An Historic Venue in Berkeley

Located at 3049 Adeline Street, the Chapel of the Flowers offers a unique experience inside a 101-year-old storybook chapel. This unique urban venue features the historic chapel plus an outdoor garden area, access to a commercial kitchen, plus a parking lot with space for for at least 25 cars or outdoor activities.

History

Originally built in 1924, this chapel has served multiple purposes over the decades. First the Hull family set up the Little Chapel of the Flowers which did funerals and also weddings. Then in 1976, it became home to Marmot Mountain Works, which sold outdoor goods in here until 2012. Then Trackers Bay Area, an adventure camp outfit for kids was in here until 2018 when they decamped. On the corner, Flaco's Tacos was a beloved staple in the community before its closure - RIP. Today, the chapel and its grounds continue as a gathering place for spiritual, cultural, and artistic events.
